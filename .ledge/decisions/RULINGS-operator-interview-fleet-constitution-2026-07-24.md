## R25 — One-codebase doctrine (authority attaches to EFFECT, not repo)

**Asked:** what agents may do in identity-plane repos before R4 is ruled.

**Selected (verbatim label):** "One-codebase doctrine."

**Binding terms:** Repo boundaries are not authority boundaries. The same grant
applies everywhere on the filesystem tree; reservations attach ONLY to runtime
effects — production, deploys, trust changes, merges to protected mains (as
classed by R29/R30). A file edit is a file edit.

**Consequences:** Cuts both ways — `auth/` inside autom8y-asana falls INSIDE
the operator gate (R29); mechanical/doc work inside the auth-service or SDK
repos falls OUTSIDE it. The inaugural wave's repo-shaped halt boundary is
retired in favor of the effect-shaped gate.

## R28 — Admissible readings of the bar ("the audit line names that human")

**Asked:** which readings of the predicate's audit line are admissible as
bar-satisfying landings for the re-derivation. Multi-select; the predicate
itself is verbatim-fixed and unamended.

**Selected (verbatim labels):** "In-the-business-record" AND "Structured log
lines." NOT selected (a ruling): "Fleet-owned durable store" and
"External-auditor grade" — EXCLUDED as the bar; they may appear in options

**Binding terms:** The audit line may land (a) written INTO the business record

**Consequences (load-bearing):** The inaugural wave's in-repo impossibility

SDK matter). The audit-landing leg comes home to this repo, which writes
stories and structured logs today. The auth-service audit-table path is dead
as the bar. Nothing in this ruling weakens R4: the species migration remains
operator-reserved.

## R29 — The identity gate (operator-word effect-classes)

**Asked:** under effect-not-repo doctrine, which effect-classes require the
operator's word to merge/deploy/flip, wherever the code lives. Multi-select.

**Selected (verbatim labels):** "Species & validators" AND "Audit semantics."
NOT selected (a ruling): "Scope enforcement" and "All auth-adjacent code."

**Binding terms:** Inside the gate: (a) token species, claims models, validator
contracts, isinstance gates — anything changing what credentials the perimeter
accepts; (b) audit semantics — what gets recorded, as whom, and where; any
change to attribution meaning. Outside the gate: scope-enforcement CODE and
mechanical auth-adjacent work (refactors, bumps) — these ride R26 discipline.

**Scribe note (carried, not asked):** R4's POLICY reservation — who may use
it — stands untouched beneath this ruling. Changing enforcement code is below
the gate; changing the policy of who may use remains the operator's alone.
